Once your audit team publishes results, you'll see them in your **Findings Dashboard**. This page explains how to read that dashboard so you can quickly understand what was found, how serious each issue is, and what it means for you.

## Finding Types

Each finding is also categorized by **type**, which tells you what kind of security issue it is:

| Type | What it means |
|------|----------------|
| **Vulnerability** | A weakness identified in your systems that could potentially be exploited by an attacker. |
| **Exploitation** | Your audit team successfully exploited a vulnerability to demonstrate real, confirmed impact — not just a theoretical risk. |
| **Threat Hypothesis** | A potential risk scenario identified by the audit team that hasn't been directly confirmed as exploitable, but is worth addressing as a precaution. |
| **Observation** | Not a vulnerability — a note, best-practice suggestion, or point worth being aware of. |
| **Achievement** | A security control or best practice that's already properly implemented — confirmed by your audit team. Not a problem, but useful to know what's already working well. |

You can filter the dashboard by type using the **Type** filter above the findings list.

<Tip>
Findings of type **Achievement** typically have an **Info** severity — they highlight what's already secure, not what needs fixing.
</Tip>

## Severity Levels

Every finding is assigned a severity level, so you can quickly understand how urgent it is.

| Severity | What it means | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **Critical** | Could let an attacker take full control of a system or access highly sensitive data right now. Needs immediate attention. | An exposed admin panel with no password protection. |
| **High** | A serious weakness that's likely exploitable and could lead to real damage. | A login page vulnerable to password-guessing attacks. |
| **Medium** | A real issue, but harder to exploit or with more limited impact. | Missing security headers that make certain attacks easier. |
| **Low** | A minor weakness, unlikely to cause harm on its own. | Outdated software version with no known active exploit. |
| **Info** | Not a vulnerability, but worth knowing — a best-practice suggestion. | Recommendation to enable additional logging. |

## CVSS Scores

Alongside severity, you may also see a **CVSS score** — a number from 0 to 10 that measures how severe a vulnerability is.

- **0.1 – 3.9** → Low
- **4.0 – 6.9** → Medium
- **7.0 – 8.9** → High
- **9.0 – 10.0** → Critical

You don't need to calculate this yourself — Rifteo shows it automatically next to each finding. Think of it as a more precise version of the severity label: two "High" findings might have different CVSS scores depending on exactly how easy they are to exploit.

## Draft vs Published

Findings go through two stages:

- **Draft** — Your audit team is still reviewing and confirming the finding. Drafts are private and only visible to the audit team.
- **Published** — The finding has been reviewed and confirmed. You'll only ever see **Published** findings in your dashboard — this ensures you're never shown an issue before it's been verified.

<Note>
If a finding isn't showing up yet, it may still be in Draft. Your audit team is likely still confirming the details before publishing it.
</Note>

## Understanding Recommendations, Risks & Compliance

Each published finding includes more than just a description of the problem:

- **Recommendation** — practical guidance from your audit team on how to fix the issue
- **Risk** — a plain-English explanation of what could happen if the issue isn't addressed
- **Compliance** — where relevant, which standards or regulations the finding relates to (e.g. GDPR, ISO 27001), so you can prioritize based on your compliance needs

This context is there to help you and your technical team decide what to fix first.

<Note>
Direct remediation actions (such as marking a finding as resolved) are currently being redesigned and aren't available yet.
If you have a question or concern about a specific finding, you can leave a comment on the audit thread to communicate directly with your audit team.
</Note>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I see a finding my auditor mentioned?">
    It's likely still in **Draft** status. Findings only appear in your dashboard once they've been reviewed and published.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What should I fix first?">
    Start with **Critical** and **High** severity findings — these carry the greatest risk. The CVSS score can help you prioritize further within the same severity level.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I mark a finding as resolved myself?">
    Not yet — this feature is currently being redesigned. In the meantime, you can leave a comment on the audit thread to update your audit team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I disagree with a finding's severity?">
    Leave a comment on the audit thread explaining your reasoning — your audit team can review and clarify.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
